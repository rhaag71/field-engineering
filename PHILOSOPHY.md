# Field Engineering Philosophy

## Purpose

This document defines the principles that guide our engineering decisions, our customer interactions, and our troubleshooting methodology.

Technical knowledge can be learned.

Professional conduct must be practiced.

---

# Priorities

1. Protect the technician and the customer.
2. Protect the customer's equipment and your equipment.
3. Do no harm. Do not introduce new faults.
4. Work methodically.

If any action conflicts with a higher priority, stop and reassess.

---

# Engineering

Engineering is the disciplined pursuit of truth.

We are not in the business of confirming assumptions.

We are in the business of discovering facts.

Measure before concluding.

Observe before theorizing.

Truth takes precedence over opinions.

Experience creates hypotheses.

Evidence creates conclusions.

---

# Troubleshooting

Symptoms are data.

Diagnoses are hypotheses.

A hypothesis is not a conclusion.

Planning for possible outcomes is encouraged.

Diagnosing equipment without evidence is not.

Unknown is an acceptable answer.

Guessing is not.

If the evidence contradicts your theory, abandon the theory.

---

# Communication

Honesty is mandatory.

Never pretend to know something you do not.

When speaking with customers:

"I don't know yet."

is followed by

"I'll add that to the list of things I need to answer."

Questions become action items.

When speaking with engineers and technicians:

"I don't know."

should naturally become

"Do you?"

or

"Let's find out."

The objective is always to discover the truth.

---

# Mistakes

Mistakes happen.

Professionals acknowledge them immediately.

Do not hide mistakes.

Do not attempt to quietly repair mistakes before informing the appropriate people.

Understand the cause.

Correct the problem.

Improve the process.

Share the lesson when appropriate.

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is to avoid making the same mistake twice.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Every difficult repair should improve both the engineer and the process.

If a mistake can be prevented with a better procedure, checklist, measurement, or tool, improve the process.

Experience is valuable.

Improved processes are permanent.
