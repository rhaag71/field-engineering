# Field Engineering Checklist

A concise pre-flight checklist for working on unfamiliar equipment in the field.

This checklist is not a training manual. It is a reminder of the habits that prevent avoidable harm, damage, wasted time, and incorrect conclusions.

---

# Priorities

1. Protect the technician and the customer.
2. Protect the customer's equipment and your equipment.
3. Do no harm. Do not introduce new faults.
4. Work methodically.

If any action conflicts with a higher priority, stop and reassess.

---

# Before Connecting

* [ ] Identify hazards.
* [ ] Determine the power source (mains, battery, solar, control power, unknown).
* [ ] Photograph wiring, jumpers, switches, and connectors before changing anything.
* [ ] Verify supply voltage.
* [ ] Verify polarity.
* [ ] Measure before connecting test equipment.
* [ ] Verify communication voltage levels before connecting USB, TTL, RS-232, RS-485, CAN, or other interfaces.
* [ ] Decide whether isolation is appropriate.

---

# During Diagnosis

* [ ] Observe symptoms before forming conclusions.
* [ ] Treat customer diagnoses as hypotheses.
* [ ] Make one change at a time.
* [ ] Record observations and measurements.
* [ ] Record every change.
* [ ] If the evidence contradicts the theory, abandon the theory.
* [ ] If something unexpected happens, stop and understand it before continuing.

---

# Power & Ground

* [ ] Prefer current-limited power when energizing unknown low-voltage equipment.
* [ ] Do not unintentionally power the target through USB.
* [ ] Verify common ground before connecting interfaces.
* [ ] Check for unexpected voltage between grounds.
* [ ] Use isolation when working with unknown, mains-referenced, or installed equipment.

---

# Before Leaving

* [ ] Restore wiring, jumpers, switches, and covers.
* [ ] Verify the system is left in a known state.
* [ ] Verify all tools, probes, and adapters have been removed.
* [ ] Communicate what is known.
* [ ] Communicate what remains unknown.
* [ ] Convert unanswered questions into follow-up action items.
* [ ] Record lessons learned while they are still fresh.
